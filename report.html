Relationship between poverty and malnutrition (2020)

Brief interpretation

The maps and scatterplot suggest that countries with higher numbers of people living in poverty tend to also report higher rates of child underweight in 2020. While the relationship is not perfectly linear, there is a visible positive association: as the count of people in poverty increases, the percentage of underweight children also tends to be higher. Exceptions and missing data exist due to reporting differences, but overall the indicators are aligned with expectations that material deprivation correlates with worse child nutrition outcomes.
